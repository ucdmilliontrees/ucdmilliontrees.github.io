We must Take Responsible Actions for the Future

The largest recorded oak in Ireland ‘Bé Binn’ has accumulated over 17,000kg of carbon over its lifetime. Using the CAVAT (Capital Asset Valuation for Amenity Trees) the tree is valued to be worth €422,209 to the community. Healthy trees consume carbon, whereas dead and decaying trees release carbon back into the atmosphere. Maintaining healthy trees in environmentally friendly ways will improve their effectiveness Ecosytem services provided by Mountshannon village trees While many tree planting initiatives focus on rural planting, urban planting should also be highly sought after and has extra benefits when planted in cities: A mature tree absorbs 54 – 108 kilograms of dust and pollutants out of the air. A 25ft tree can reduce annual heating and cooling in a residence by 8 – 12% Urban Forest Values Some things to think about when planting urban trees: Large leafs on average increase shade and pollution removal. High maintenance trees and ones that require a lot of water in drought areas should be avoided. Trees that are likely to increase pollen count should also be limited in urban environments where alternatives are available. Planting Healthy Air - The Nature Conservancy
